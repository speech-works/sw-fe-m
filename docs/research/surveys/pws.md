# Survey Questions — People Who Stutter (PWS)

Primary end users (adults, self-directed). See [`README.md`](README.md) for conventions.

| ID | Question | Type | Informs | Priority | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PWS-001 | Would you invite a friend or family member to practice with you in the app? | yes/no | Referral loop — is the loop viable at all | P0 | draft |
| PWS-002 | If you'd invite someone, who would it most likely be? (a) another person who stutters (b) a close friend (c) a partner/spouse (d) a parent/family (e) my therapist | multiple-choice | **Forks the eventual pairing model: peer vs supporter.** See `referral-loop-spec.md` §11 | P0 | draft |
| PWS-003 | Stuttering can feel private. How comfortable are you sharing that you use a *stuttering-practice* app with someone you'd invite? (1 = very uncomfortable, 5 = totally fine) | scale | Referral loop — **stigma is the #1 risk to invite rate**; may need an anonymous/neutral framing | P0 | draft |
| PWS-004 | Would "you both get a free month" make you more likely to invite someone? | yes/no | Referral reward design (two-sided incentive) | P1 | draft |
| PWS-005 | What would stop you from inviting someone, even if you wanted to? | open | Referral loop — surface friction we haven't modeled | P1 | draft |
| PWS-006 | Would having a practice partner / accountability buddy help you stick with daily practice? | scale | Pairing — demand validation before building the social graph | P1 | draft |
| PWS-007 | If you had a partner, what would you want to share with them? (a) just that I practiced (streak) (b) my progress stats (c) nothing — keep it private (d) practice together live | multiple-choice | Pairing — how much to share; privacy defaults | P1 | draft |
| PWS-008 | Would a guided story (3–4 connected prompts building one narrative) appeal more than single one-off speaking prompts? | multiple-choice | Guided Storytelling Sequences — validate the format | P1 | draft |
| PWS-009 | When you practice reading aloud, does a *time/speed* element help you or make you tense up? | multiple-choice | **Speed-reading game** — does timing trigger rushing for you? (pairs with THER-001) | P1 | draft |
| PWS-010 | Would a voice-controlled mini-game (say "left/right/jump" to move a character) appeal to you as practice? | scale | Direction game — gauge adult appeal (currently deprioritized for adults) | P2 | draft |
| PWS-011 | If a game required clean speech to respond, and ignored you when you blocked/repeated, how would that feel? | open | Direction game — confirms the disfluency-tolerance design requirement | P1 | draft |
| PWS-012 | What's the single biggest reason you'd keep coming back to a speech app daily? | open | Retention — the denominator that protects the referral loop | P1 | draft |
